# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## Change Manager

### Role Summary
Change Managers oversee and coordinate all changes to project scope, schedule, and deliverables. They ensure change requests are properly evaluated, approved, and communicated across all stakeholders.

### Responsibilities
- Review and assess change requests for impact on scope, timeline, and resources
- Coordinate change approval processes with stakeholders
- Maintain change logs and ensure transparent communication of changes
- Work with Project Managers to update project plans reflecting approved changes
- Facilitate change control board meetings when needed

### Goals
- Minimize disruption from uncontrolled changes
- Ensure all changes are properly evaluated and documented
- Maintain project stability while enabling necessary adaptability

### Typical Communication
- Change request forms and impact assessments
- Change approval notifications and status updates
- Change log reviews with Project Managers
- Stakeholder briefings on significant changes

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Project Managers**: Partners closely to assess change impacts on timelines and resources
- **Product Managers**: Collaborates on scope changes and feature prioritization adjustments
- **Developers**: Communicates technical implications of approved changes
- **Stakeholder Champions**: Coordinates on stakeholder-driven change requests

---

## Risk Analyst

### Role Summary
Risk Analysts proactively identify, evaluate, and monitor project risks. They develop mitigation strategies and ensure risk awareness across the project team.

### Responsibilities
- Continuously identify potential project risks (technical, schedule, resource, external)
- Assess risk likelihood and impact using established frameworks
- Develop and propose risk mitigation and contingency strategies
- Maintain risk registers and provide regular risk status updates
- Escalate high-priority risks to Project Managers and stakeholders

### Goals
- Identify risks early before they become issues
- Reduce the probability and impact of negative events
- Increase project predictability and resilience

### Typical Communication
- Risk registers and risk assessment matrices
- Regular risk status reports and dashboards
- Risk mitigation proposals and action plans
- Escalation briefings for critical risks

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Project Managers**: Primary partner for risk planning and escalation decisions
- **Developers**: Sources technical risk inputs and validates technical mitigations
- **Product Managers**: Collaborates on product-related and market risks
- **Compliance Leads**: Coordinates on regulatory and compliance risks

---

## Stakeholder Champion

### Role Summary
Stakeholder Champions serve as the primary liaison between the project team and key external stakeholders. They advocate for stakeholder needs while maintaining alignment with project goals.

### Responsibilities
- Act as single point of contact for assigned stakeholder groups
- Gather, synthesize, and communicate stakeholder feedback to the team
- Ensure stakeholder expectations are understood and managed appropriately
- Facilitate stakeholder review sessions and feedback loops
- Advocate for stakeholder interests in planning and monitor stakeholder satisfaction

### Goals
- Maximize stakeholder satisfaction and engagement
- Ensure stakeholder voice is represented in project decisions
- Prevent misalignment between project direction and stakeholder needs
- Build and maintain strong stakeholder relationships

### Typical Communication
- Stakeholder briefings and status updates
- Feedback summaries and requirements clarifications
- Stakeholder meeting facilitation and follow-ups
- Stakeholder satisfaction surveys and reports

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Product Managers**: Partners to ensure stakeholder needs inform product decisions
- **Project Managers**: Collaborates on stakeholder communication plans and timing
- **Change Managers**: Coordinates on stakeholder-driven change requests
- **Developers**: Bridges communication between technical team and non-technical stakeholders

---

## Compliance Lead

### Role Summary
Compliance Leads ensure project activities, deliverables, and processes adhere to organizational policies, legal requirements, industry standards, and relevant regulations.

### Responsibilities
- Identify applicable compliance requirements (regulatory, security, privacy, organizational)
- Review project plans and deliverables for compliance adherence
- Provide guidance and training to team members on compliance topics
- Maintain compliance documentation and audit trails
- Coordinate with legal, security, and audit teams as needed
- Flag and escalate compliance risks or violations

### Goals
- Ensure 100% compliance with applicable regulations and standards
- Minimize compliance-related project delays or rework
- Build compliance awareness and capability within project teams
- Protect organization from legal and regulatory risks

### Typical Communication
- Compliance checklists and assessment reports
- Training materials and compliance guidelines
- Audit documentation and evidence logs
- Compliance status updates and risk escalations

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Project Managers**: Integrates compliance requirements into project plans and schedules
- **Developers**: Provides guidance on secure coding, data handling, and technical compliance
- **Risk Analysts**: Collaborates to identify and mitigate compliance-related risks
- **Product Managers**: Ensures product features meet regulatory requirements
